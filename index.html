Uplistsikhe

Uplistsikhe is an ancient rock-hewn city dating back to the Early Iron Age. It was a major political and religious center before the advent of Christianity and is famous for its unique blend of pagan and early Christian architecture. The labyrinth of tunnels, temples, and streets carved into the rock reveals sophisticated urban planning of ancient Georgia.

Narikala Fortress

Overlooking the capital city of Tbilisi, Narikala Fortress is one of Georgia's oldest fortresses, originally built in the 4th century. Expanded by Arabs and later Georgian kings, it served as a strategic defense post. Visitors admire its panoramic views and the nearby statue of Kartlis Deda, symbolizing the spirit of the Georgian people.

Prometheus Caves

Located near the town of Tskaltubo, the Prometheus Caves are one of Georgia’s largest and most spectacular cave systems. These caves showcase fascinating stalactites, stalagmites, underground rivers, and ancient fossils, making them a striking natural and historical attraction tied to the region’s geological past.

Svan Towers

The Svaneti region is famous for its medieval stone towers, known as Svan Towers. Built between the 9th and 12th centuries, they were used for family defense and symbolize the mountainous region’s unique cultural heritage. The towers stand as steadfast guardians of Georgia’s ancient highland traditions and warrior spirit.

Vardzia Cave Monastery

Vardzia is a remarkable 12th-century cave monastery complex carved into the cliffs of southern Georgia. Serving as a spiritual fortress during Queen Tamar’s reign, it features rock-cut churches, frescoes, and tunnels. This site reflects Georgia’s medieval religious devotion and architectural ingenuity.

Mtskheta

Mtskheta, one of Georgia’s oldest cities, was once the capital and is the country’s spiritual heart. Its UNESCO World Heritage churches like Svetitskhoveli Cathedral and Jvari Monastery represent the cradle of Georgian Orthodox Christianity dating back over 1,500 years.

Gergeti Trinity Church

Nestled under the towering Mount Kazbek, the Gergeti Trinity Church is a stunning 14th-century church perched on a hilltop. It is an iconic symbol of Georgia’s mountainous heritage as well as its Christian resilience, featuring breathtaking views of the Caucasus Mountains.
